Overview

Speaker Workshop is a Windows based program designed to test speaker drivers and design speakers. The basic file type in Speaker Workshop (the document) is a project tree containing many different basic types (such as folders, drivers, enclosures) called resources.

Using a standard sound card, speaker workshop can test and measure:

Driver Impedance Test a driver’s impedance from 5Hz to 20Khz with a resolution of 1/3 of a Hz
Passive Components Find the value of a passive component to within approximately 1%. It also finds the Q of inductors.
Transfer Function The frequency response / transfer function of an amplifier can be tested from 5Hz to 20Khz with a resolution of 1/3 Hz.
Driver Acoustic Response With a suitable microphone, test acoustic response of a driver over the same ranges as above.
Harmonic Distortion Measure harmonic distortion in speakers and amplifiers, down to approximately .03%.
Intermodulation Distortion Measure intermodulation distortion down to approximately .03%.

The design capabilities of Speaker Workshop let you design:

Enclosures Design sealed and vented enclosures for woofers and midranges using a range of Q and tuning values.
Stock Crossovers Insert stock crossovers and standard impedance corrections (inductive rise or resonant peak).
Custom Crossovers Includes full crossover design capabilities for designing complex crossovers. With calculated impedance and acoustic results.

Speaker Workshop comes with a signal generator module capable of generating 10 types of signals, as well as any custom signal defined by a .WAV file.

Speaker Workshop has a wide range of analytical tools including:

Transformations Including FFT, Inverse FFT, arithmetic, smoothing, splicing, and digital filtering.
Charts Charts can include multiple datasets. Position information for each chart is easily displayed. Zoom-in and out are supported.
