Warble tab (Signal Properties dialog)

This page contains settings for the Warble type of signal. A warble is a type of sweep that goes backwards and forwards through a range repeatedly. A warble of about 1/3 octave is often used in acoustic room measurement with a sound level meter. Fields are:

Center Frequency The frequency around which the warble is based.
Octave Spread The entire range of the warble. It is split 50/50 on each side of the center.
Warble Rate How many times the warble occurs per second.
