A standard cable is a cable that connects the left channel output (or, if you are using an amplifier - the amplifier output) to the device under test. In the case of the device under test, just put two large alligator clips at one end or solder that end to two large binding posts. A standard input cable has three large alligator clips (ground, left, right). This is used for low-voltage testing.

For an interesting overview, see Cables and Connectors
